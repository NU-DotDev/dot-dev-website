What We Do

While we’re mostly known for our projects program, you don’t have to work on a project to be involved in .dev. We try to foster a community for tech enthusiasts of all levels by hosting a variety of events throughout the year, from technical workshops to movie nights!

Projects

Whether you have a great app idea that you want to pursue or you’re just looking for hands-on development experience, consider joining .projects! Participants get the option of receiving a mentor to guide their teams throughout the year before final presentations at the end of spring.

Interview Prep

We understand that recruitment can be a stressful time of year for our members. Through LeetCode sessions and internship panels throughout the year, we ensure that our members feel prepared in their job searches without cramming the night before an interview.

Technical Workshops

Your CS classes don’t always teach you the skills you need to build an app or a website. That’s where our workshops come in. Guest speakers or members of exec take turns introducing curious members to relevant topics in programming, like using Git or building an AWS serverless application.

UI/UX Design

If you’re more interested in the communication or design areas of tech, there’s a place at .dev for you too! At our UI/UX workshops, you’ll [take a step back from the deep code-heavy recesses of your application and] learn how to build mockups and create user-friendly designs.

Hackathon

Our annual hackathon gives participants a chance to network with companies, collaborate with other programmers and practice building an innovative project under a strict time constraint.

Social Events

To facilitate bonding in the .dev community, we host a variety of events throughout the year, from boba socials to Smash tournaments.

FAQ's

Frequently Asked Questions & Answers

Q: Do I have to be experienced in software development to join?

A: No, we accept both beginners and experienced individuals who share a common drive to learn and contribute!

Q: What if I don’t know how to code?

A: Not a problem! .dev is a safe place to explore software development. Many of our workshops assume no technical experience, such as UI/UX design. And if you want to try your hand at software, we’re happy to provide you with workshops and resources to get started.

Q: Northwestern seems to have a lot of tech clubs. How do I know which one is right for me?

A: Don’t feel pressured to decide! Student organizations are by no means mutually exclusive. Our club roster has lots of crossover with EPIC, WiC and IEEE, and we try to host joint social events when possible to make the Northwestern tech community more cohesive.
